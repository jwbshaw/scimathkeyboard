Installation

  1. Download SciMath Keyboard from the App Store.
  2. Open Settings → General → Keyboard → Keyboards → Add New Keyboard.
  3. Select SciMath Keyboard from the list.
  4. When prompted about Full Access, you do not need to enable it. SciMath works without it.
  5. Switch to SciMath at any time using the globe key on your keyboard.

Switching Keyboards

Tap the globe key to cycle to the next keyboard. Hold the globe key to see a list of all installed keyboards and pick one directly.

Layers

LayerAccessContents
ABCDefault / ABC buttonQWERTY letters. Long-press any letter for diacritical variants.
123123 buttonNumbers and common punctuation.
#+= #+= button (from 123)Brackets, math operators, currency symbols.
scisci button (from #+=)Scientific and mathematical symbols.

The layer cycle is: 123 → #+= → sci → 123. The ABC button returns to letters from any layer.

Slide-to-Type

Slide your finger across keys to type without lifting. The character under your finger when you lift is inserted. Dwelling on a key for about ¾ of a second opens the diacritical popup.

iPad Pull-Down

On iPad, each key shows a small secondary character above the main label. Swipe down on any key to insert that secondary character without switching layers.

Trackpad Mode

Hold the spacebar until the key labels fade. You are now in trackpad mode — drag left or right to move the text cursor. Lift your finger to exit.

Spacebar

The spacebar flashes "Scientific" briefly each time the keyboard appears. The sci badge in the lower right is a permanent language indicator. Key flash (brief colour inversion on tap) can be toggled in the SciMath app.

Dictation

SciMath does not provide dictation. Dictation requires Full Access and sends voice to Apple's servers. If you need dictation, switch to a stock system keyboard, dictate, then switch back to SciMath.

Recommended Workflow for Extended Typing

For long prose passages, use your home-language keyboard (EN, IS, etc.) and switch to SciMath when you need scientific or symbol input, then switch back. This is particularly relevant on iOS 26.x where a known Apple platform issue affects third-party keyboard accuracy under rapid typing.

Full Access

SciMath does not require Full Access and will never ask for it. iOS displays a standard warning when you add any third-party keyboard. This warning does not reflect SciMath's actual behaviour — SciMath never logs or transmits what you type.
